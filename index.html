Madison Valley Wildlife Drive

Cruise slowly east along the Madison River toward Madison Junction. This is one of the best easy wildlife corridors in the park — bison herds graze in the open meadows and elk are common, especially in the low morning light. Drive slowly, pull over often. No hiking required here; let the landscape come to you.

Hike · 5.4 mi RT · ~100 ft gain

Fairy Falls & Grand Prismatic Overlook

Drive south from Madison toward the Midway Geyser Basin area and hike the Fairy Falls Trail. The trail is nearly flat for most of its length — it follows an old service road through open lodgepole forest — with a short, steep climb of roughly 100 feet up to the Grand Prismatic Overlook. From there you get a breathtaking aerial view of the spring's vivid color rings before descending back to the main trail and continuing to Fairy Falls, a gorgeous 200-foot waterfall. Arriving this early means you'll likely have the overlook nearly to yourselves. This is the visual centerpiece of the day.

  • Short steep climb (~100 ft) delivers an aerial view of Grand Prismatic Spring — arrive early to have it nearly to yourselves
  • Trail continues to Fairy Falls, a 200-foot waterfall tucked at the end of the path

Old Faithful & Upper Geyser Basin

Drive south to Old Faithful (~20 min). Check the predicted eruption time at the visitor center and time your arrival for a show. The boardwalk loop around the Upper Geyser Basin is worth a quick stroll — it's the densest collection of geysers anywhere in the world, and there's usually enough space early morning to feel comfortable.

  • Check the eruption schedule at the visitor center immediately on arrival — time your viewing before walking the basin
  • Upper Geyser Basin boardwalk: densest geyser field on earth, most comfortable before the mid-morning crowds arrive

Hike · 4.8 mi RT · ~100 ft gain

Lone Star Geyser Trail

One of the best crowd-avoidance moves in the park. The trail follows the Firehole River on a gentle, mostly paved old service road — it gains only about 100 feet of elevation over the full 2.4 miles out, making it accessible for everyone in the group. The payoff is a backcountry geyser cone with virtually no other visitors. Lone Star erupts roughly every 3 hours and shoots ~45 feet — a trail log at the cone records the last eruption time, so you can judge whether to wait. Bring lunch to eat along the river.

  • Feels like backcountry — virtually no other visitors; trail log at the cone shows the last eruption time
  • Lone Star erupts ~every 3 hours and shoots 45 ft; worth a 20–30 min wait if the timing lines up

Canyon

Grand Canyon of Yellowstone

Drive straight through Hayden Valley from Fishing Bridge and head directly to Canyon — arriving at 1 PM puts you there before the full Friday afternoon wave hits. The South Rim Trail is an easy 1-mile walk to Artist Point, with its iconic view of the Lower Falls plunging into the yellow-walled canyon. If the group has energy, the Uncle Tom's Trail drops roughly 300 feet via steep metal staircases (about 328 steps) to a platform near the base of the falls — you'll earn that climb back up, but the close-up view is worth every step.

  • South Rim Trail to Artist Point: easy 1-mile walk with the park's most iconic overlook of the Lower Falls
  • Uncle Tom's Trail (optional): 328 steep metal steps, ~300 ft descent to a platform near the base of the falls

Hayden Valley Drive

From Canyon, backtrack south — the 15-mile drive back through Hayden Valley takes about 20 minutes and drops you right into the late afternoon window when bison, wolves, and bears are at their most active. This timing is actually better than midday: animals move more in the cooler afternoon light, and the angle of the sun makes the valley floor glow. Drive slowly, windows down, and scan the hillsides. Pull over for any wildlife jam — this is exactly what you came for.

  • Late afternoon = peak wildlife activity; bison nearly guaranteed, wolves and grizzlies occasionally spotted
  • Cooler temps and angled light make this the best wildlife viewing window of the entire day

Norris Geyser Basin → West Yellowstone

From Hayden Valley, head north back through Canyon junction and then west to Norris — about 25 miles and 35 minutes. Norris is the hottest and most dynamic thermal basin in the park, with a slightly less touristy feel than Old Faithful. The Back Basin loop (1.5 miles) passes Steamboat Geyser, the world's tallest active geyser. From Norris, it's 28 miles back to West Yellowstone via Madison. Arrive back at the lodge by 5:30 PM.

  • Hottest thermal basin in the park; Back Basin loop (1.5 mi) passes Steamboat Geyser — world's tallest active geyser

Thermal

Norris Geyser Basin

The hottest, most acidic, and most dynamic thermal basin in the park. Temperatures here reach 459°F just below the surface — the plumbing is still actively evolving, so features change year to year in ways you don't see elsewhere. Walk the Porcelain Basin loop (0.75 miles, flat and open — a stark white silica landscape) and then head into the Back Basin loop (1.5 miles) to find Steamboat Geyser, the world's tallest active geyser at 300–400 feet when it fully erupts. It doesn't erupt on a schedule, but the cone and the surrounding features make the walk worth it regardless.

  • Back Basin loop (1.5 mi): passes Steamboat Geyser — world's tallest active geyser
  • Porcelain Basin loop (0.75 mi): bright open silica flats, good if time is short

Canyon

Grand Canyon of Yellowstone

Drive east 12 miles from Norris to Canyon Village (~20 min). The South Rim Trail is an easy 1-mile walk to Artist Point — the most photographed spot in the park, with its iconic view of the Lower Falls plunging 308 feet into the vivid yellow-walled canyon. The walls get their color from iron oxides and hydrothermal alteration — the same chemistry driving everything you've seen on the trip. If the group has energy, Uncle Tom's Trail drops roughly 300 feet via steep metal staircases (328 steps) to a platform near the base of the falls. Allow 1.5–2 hours to do both properly.

  • South Rim Trail to Artist Point: easy 1-mile walk, the park's most iconic viewpoint
  • Uncle Tom's Trail (optional): 328 steep metal steps, ~300 ft descent to near the base of the falls

Wildlife

Hayden Valley → Return Home

Drive south 15 miles from Canyon along the Yellowstone River into Hayden Valley. This is 50 square miles of open meadow and rolling hills — bison are nearly always present, and this is the most reliable spot in the park for seeing wolves and grizzlies from the roadside. Mid-morning activity is solid; animals are still moving before the midday heat. Drive the full length of the valley slowly and scan the hillsides. From the south end, turn back north and return via Canyon Junction → Norris → Madison → West Yellowstone. You'll be back at the lodge by approximately 2:15–2:30 PM.

  • Best wolf and grizzly corridor in the park — scan hillsides on both sides
  • Return route: Hayden → Canyon → Norris → Madison → West Yellowstone (~70 miles, ~1h 20min)

Before You Go

  • 🐻 Carry bear spray for every hike — you'll be in active grizzly habitat all day. Know how to use it before you need it.
  • 📵 Cell service is nearly nonexistent inside the park. Screenshot this itinerary or download an offline map (Maps.me or Gaia GPS work well) before you enter.
  • 🚐 One van means one parking spot — a huge advantage. Trailhead lots are small, so arriving early protects your spot as much as it protects your sanity.
  • 🚗 The park speed limit is 45 mph (often slower near wildlife), and unexpected jams can add 20–40 minutes. Embrace them — that's the best wildlife viewing.
  • 🎟️ The park entrance fee is $35 per vehicle for a 7-day pass — pay at the West Entrance booth or buy in advance at recreation.gov to skip the line entirely.
  • 💧 Carry at least 2 liters of water per person — no refill stations on the trails. Pack more food than you think you need for seven people.
